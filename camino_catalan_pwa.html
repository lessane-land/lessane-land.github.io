Tramuntana Wind Warning

The Alt Empordà is famous for the tramuntana — a cold, powerful north wind that can blow at 80–100 km/h. It can appear suddenly, especially in autumn and winter. It's beautiful and brutal in equal measure.

Best seasons to walk
🌸
BEST
Spring (Apr–May)
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Perfect temperatures 16–22°C. Wildflowers on the Cap de Creus. Green fields. Best light for the monastery. Some rain possible.
🍂
BEST
Autumn (Sep–Oct)
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Golden light, harvest season, fewer tourists. Temperatures 18–24°C. Tramuntana risk increases in October. Ideal overall.
☀️
Summer (Jun–Aug)
⭐⭐⭐
Very hot 28–35°C. Exposed descents brutal in midday sun. Start walking by 6am. Carry extra water. Dalí Museum very crowded.
❄️
Winter (Nov–Mar)
⭐⭐
Very few pilgrims. Cold 5–12°C. Strong tramuntana likely. Some services closed. Beautiful solitude but challenging logistics.
Monthly temperature guide
Packing by weather
👟
FootwearTrail running shoes work great on this route — lighter than heavy boots. The descent to Pau is the only truly technical section. Waterproof recommended Oct–Apr.
🧴
Sun protection (critical in summer)SPF 50 sunscreen, sun hat with neck cover, and sunglasses are non-negotiable Jun–Sep. The descent to Pau is completely exposed.
🌬️
Windproof layerAlways carry a light windproof jacket. The tramuntana arrives without warning and can drop the felt temperature by 10°C instantly.
💧
HydrationMinimum 2L capacity from the monastery to Vilajuïga (15 km, no water). In summer, carry 3L. Electrolyte tablets recommended.
